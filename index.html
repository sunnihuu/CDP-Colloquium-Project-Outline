Initial Idea

How Does Food Travel and Transform?

"The delicious rice noodles we are familiar with today, the beef pho with mint, cilantro, Thai basil, lime, and chili, each step in its developmental history is intertwined with the heartbreaking journey of Vietnamese refugees escaping from North to South Vietnam, and then from East to West.” -- Yu Feng

Rising Household Food Costs

From 2012 to 2023, the cost of food at home increased by 65.8%, rising faster than dining out.

Source: New York State Office of the State Comptroller, 2025

Food Deserts in NYC

About 3 million NYC residents live in neighborhoods with poor access to grocery stores.

Source: food-deserts.com

Food Insecurity & Supply Gaps

Meal gaps and food supply shortages contribute to health issues and deepen social inequality.

Source: Food Metrics Report 2021- Food Policy NYC

A Global Challenge

As global food demand grows, the entire food system faces rising pressure. This strain fuels competition across supply chains and drives unsustainable production practices.

Source: Oxford Martin Programme on the Future of Food

Food → Climate

Food production is a major driver of climate change, contributing to extreme weather events that, in turn, reduce crop yields, creating a vicious cycle.

Source: foodprint.org

Climate → Food

Extreme weather reduces crop yields, shrinking food supply and driving up prices, adding pressure to already fragile food systems.

Source: Time Magazine

foodprint

The total environmental and social impact of food production and consumption: including carbon emissions, water usage, land use, and ethical or social dimensions.

Source: ourworldindata.orgs

A Major Urban Impact

Food is the 3rd largest material and carbon flow in cities. In 80% of cities, food accounts for 10%–20% of total emissions, driven both by production and consumption habits.

Source: Goldstein, Benjamin et al. "Surveying the Environmental Footprint of Urban Food Consumption." © 2016 Yale University

Part 1: Computational Process

  1. Data Collection & Classification
    • Tools: Python (Pandas, GeoPandas), Rhino/Grasshopper
    • Method: Extract and categorize restaurants by cuisine type; link each to a carbon intensity category.
  2. Carbon Emissions Modeling
    • Tools: Python, Excel (for carbon matrix modeling)
    • Method: Apply per-meal carbon intensity to cuisine types using:
      Foodprint (kg CO₂e) = Number of Meals × Emission Factor
  3. Spatial Aggregation & Visualization
    • Tools: Grasshopper, QGIS, Mapbox Studio
    • Method: Aggregate emissions at tract or borough level; generate comparative carbon intensity maps.
  4. Interactive Storytelling & UX Prototyping
    • Tools: Mapbox GL JS, D3.js, Scrollama.js, Figma
    • Method: Build a scroll-driven narrative interface; allow users to filter and explore foodprint by neighborhood or cuisine.

• The Health-Motivated Explorer

Nadia, 52 - Brooklyn, NY

Background: Public school teacher with Type 2 diabetes

Pain Points:

  • Difficult to find restaurants with health-conscious options
  • Menus lack transparency on sugar, carbs, and processed ingredients
  • Unaware that sustainable food choices can support medical goals

Motivation:

  • Maintain a diet aligned with her health condition
  • Discover plant-based or low-impact meals that also benefit her body
  • Use digital tools to filter meals by nutrition and footprint
  • Explore eating habits that support both wellness and sustainability

• The Independent Restaurateur

Luis, 35 - Manhattan, NY

Background: Owner and chef of a small independent restaurant

Pain Points:

  • Unsure how to calculate or communicate menu sustainability
  • Wants to attract younger, eco-conscious customers but lacks the tools
  • Competes with larger brands with better marketing budgets

Motivation:

  • Improve restaurant's sustainable image through menu transparency
  • Use data to adjust high-impact dishes and highlight low-footprint items
  • Display a "low-carbon certified" label or embed tools for customers
  • Join a public map of eco-friendly dining to gain visibility

• The Budget-Conscious Community Member

Victor, 41 - Queens, NY

Background: Father of three, working-class family

Pain Points:

  • Rising food costs and frequent household waste
  • Limited access to affordable, healthy options
  • Unfamiliar with foodprint but interested in practical benefits

Motivation:

  • Save money and reduce food waste through community fridges or leftover deals
  • Discover affordable meals with clear footprint feedback
  • Encourage family behavior change through small rewards or progress tracking
  • Feel part of something bigger through everyday climate-positive choices
