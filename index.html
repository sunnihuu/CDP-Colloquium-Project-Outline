Rising Household Food Costs

From 2012 to 2023, the cost of food at home increased by 65.8%, rising faster than dining out.

Source: New York State Office of the State Comptroller, 2025

Food Deserts in NYC

About 3 million NYC residents live in neighborhoods with poor access to grocery stores.

Source: food-deserts.com

Food Insecurity & Supply Gaps

Meal gaps and food supply shortages contribute to health issues and deepen social inequality.

Source: Food Metrics Report 2021- Food Policy NYC

A Global Challenge

As global food demand grows, the entire food system faces rising pressure. This strain fuels competition across supply chains and drives unsustainable production practices.

Source: Oxford Martin Programme on the Future of Food

Food → Climate

Food production is a major driver of climate change, contributing to extreme weather events that, in turn, reduce crop yields, creating a vicious cycle.

Source: foodprint.org

Climate → Food

Extreme weather reduces crop yields, shrinking food supply and driving up prices, adding pressure to already fragile food systems.

Source: Time Magazine

Foodprint

The total environmental and social impact of food production and consumption: including carbon emissions, water usage, land use, and ethical or social dimensions.

Source: ourworldindata.orgs

A Major Urban Impact

Food is the 3rd largest material and carbon flow in cities. In 80% of cities, food accounts for 10%–20% of total emissions, driven both by production and consumption habits.

Source: Goldstein, Benjamin et al. "Surveying the Environmental Footprint of Urban Food Consumption." © 2016 Yale University

"Food security exists when all people, at all times, have physical and economic access to sufficient, safe and nutritious food that meets their dietary needs and food preferences for an active and healthy life"   (World Food Summit, 1996)

Understanding the Dual Systems

🌡️ Climate Impact Chain

Climate change creates extreme weather events that reduce agricultural yields, leading to urban food shortages. This forces system expansion to meet demand, generating more emissions and accelerating climate change.

🍽️ Urban Food Cycle

The urban food system moves through production, processing, transport, retail, consumption, and waste. Each stage generates emissions and resource demands that feed back into the system's expansion.

🔗 Critical Bridge Points

The two systems intersect at key points: where food shortages drive retail demand, and where production directly contributes to emissions. These bridges represent the most strategic intervention opportunities.

Breaking the Feedback Loops

Two Interconnected Systems Drive Urban Food Insecurity:

🌡️ Climate Feedback Loop

Climate change → extreme weather → reduced yields → urban food shortages → system expansion → higher emissions

🍽️ Food System Loop

Production → processing → transport → retail → consumption → waste → back to production

How Might We...

• interrupt the climate feedback loop that drives urban food shortages?

• redesign food system flows to reduce emissions and waste?

• strengthen the bridge connections between climate resilience and food security?
